Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich, savory fillings such as seasoned ground meat, tomato sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese, typically mozzarella and Parmesan. Baked to perfection, it features a golden, bubbling top and a hearty, flavorful interior. Lasagna is known for its comforting, indulgent nature and is often served at family gatherings or special occasions. Variations exist around the world, including vegetarian versions with spinach and ricotta or regional twists with different meats and sauces. Its layered structure and rich taste make it a beloved staple in Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or sausage
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Eggs
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Italian seasoning (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions; drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, sauté until translucent.
  4. Add ground beef or sausage, cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  5. Stir in tomato sauce, season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with eggs, salt, and pepper.
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella cheese, and more meat sauce. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  8. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until cheese is bubbly and golden.
  9. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
